WILD YEAR IN REVIEW

Mammals made up 23.6% of our patients. 

Many of them came to us as tiny babies and grew up in our care.

Reptiles made up 0.6% of our patients.

Species include gopher snakes, western pond turtles, alligator lizards, and more!

Dr. Avery Berkowitz joined the team in April 2020 and has been saving lives ever since. 

Along with veterinary technicians Tema and Hadley, Avery has been establishing life-saving animal care protocols, performing orthopedic surgeries, and equipping the Wildlife Care Center with medical equipment to save wild lives like never before. The addition of an onsite wildlife veterinarian is a game-changer for wildlife on California's Central Coast! Avery is looking forward to the construction and completion of the Wildlife Hospital so they can save even more lives.

SBWCN volunteers logged 15,865 hours this year!

The true heroes of 2020 were our volunteers. When the pandemic grew serious just as Baby Season was beginning, we knew we couldn't have our full volunteer force onsite caring for the animals. But the staff and volunteers at SBWCN are rehabbers, and rehabbers find a way. Volunteers prepared diets, cleaned syringes, and answered the HELPline at home. Others safely rescued and transported animals. Some masked and gloved up, and performed socially distanced animal care. 4,697 animals got the quality care they needed thanks to our star-studded volunteer team.
Thank you, volunteers!

Thank you for supporting Santa Barbara Wildlife Care Network. You are making a difference for wildlife.

Santa Barbara Care Network is a nonprofit, volunteer organization that rescues and rehabilitates injured and orphaned wildlife, including birds, small mammals and reptiles. We are supported entirely by donations and volunteers. We are licensed by the United States Fish and Wildlife Service and California Department of Fish and Game. Our staff and volunteers currently help thousands of animals each year.
